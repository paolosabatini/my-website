President Meloni: the quiet wave


In recent years, Italian politics has been dominated by one name: Giorgia Meloni. Despite being long overlooked by political rivals, she went on to become Italy's first female Prime Minister, leading a government formed on October 23rd, 2022.

This article offers a brief analysis of Giorgia Meloni's social media activity on Twitter over the past five years (using snsscrape Python package), from the previous Italian political election to her recent win. The analysis looks not only at the frequency of her tweets but also the content, in order to gain insight into the tactics employed by Meloni and her social media team to achieve her recent election victory.



A bit of context: Italian politics 2018-22


The political landscape in Italy over the past five years has seen several changes. In the 2018 election, the M5S party, led by Luigi di Maio, won with around one third of the votes, while the Lega party (right-wing) and the Democratic Party (centre-left) received similar percentages. The first coalition government, known as "Conte I", was formed between the Lega and M5S parties with Giuseppe Conte as Prime Minister.

During the initial 18 months of the government, both participating parties engaged in their symbolic battles, including the implementation of a citizenship salary (M5S), reforms to the pension system and measures against immigration (Lega), which ultimately led to a rise in popularity for the Lega party.

However, in the summer of 2019, Salvini broke the coalition in an attempt to secure a single-party government, but this plan was overruled by the M5S and PD coalition in the autumn of 2019, leading to the formation of the "Conte II" government. This government faced the COVID crisis and the following economic crisis. In the end of 2021, a parliamentary crisis within the centre-left parties led to the President of the Republic, Mattarella, forming a broad coalition government led by former European bank President, Mario Draghi. Meloni's party, Fratelli d'Italia, was not involved in the coalition.

Following M5S's decision to exit the governing coalition in July 2022 due to their support of Ukraine against Russian aggression, Draghi resigned, and President Mattarella called for new elections in October, that saw a condensed campaign period, a successful moment for Meloni's party.

Evolution of tweet contents



From the perspective of activity, we observed Meloni taking a low-key approach. To analyze the contents of tweets, we employed several open-source tools available on the web. The first tool was TreeTagger, which associates each word in a tweet with its type (article, verb, noun) and lemma (i.e. root word). This allowed us to clean up the tweet content. Next, we used the bag-of-words (from NLTK) approach to parameterize the tweet content by replacing it with the occurrence of words in the tweet. Finally, we employed k-means clustering (from scikit-learn), optimizing the number of clusters using the silhouette and elbow methods, to group the different tweets into clusters based on word occurrences and their correlations. The 40 clusters were eventually merged into 7 thematic areas:

  • COVID-19 related tweets.
  • Immigration related tweets.
  • Italy first tweets (i.e. protectionism and nationalism content).
  • Political messages (i.e. topics related to politics, such as economy, jobs, pensions, healthcare, etc.).
  • Political opposition (i.e. direct attacks against opposition parties).
  • Self-promotion (i.e. events or slogans related to the party "Fratelli d'Italia").
  • Wishes (i.e. random wishes such as "have a good Labor Day" or "Merry Christmas").
